About Me

I am an Vice-Chancellor Assistant Professor at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK), Department of Psychiatry. Previously, I was a postdoctoral research fellow at Harvard-MGB's AI in Medicine Program. Before that, I received a PhD in Information from University of Michigan, School of Information (UMSI) and a BS in Statistics from University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA).

As part of the Department of Psychiatry, Faculty of Medicine at CUHK, my research is dedicated to advancing Medical Artificial Intelligence and Health Informatics, with a focus on Digital Mental Health and AI-driven Psychiatry. I am committed to fostering interdisciplinary collaborations between the AI community and medical and healthcare professionals. To better facilitate mutual understanding, let me outline my primary research interests from two complementary perspectives.

From the AI perspective, my research interests include:

  • Clinical Reasoning with Large AI Models. With the rapid advancement of intelligent models—including deep learning, large language models (LLMs), multimodal foundation models, and energy-based models—there is significant potential to enhance complex clinical decision-making processes. My research aims to develop clinically applicable AI models, with a particular emphasis on Psychiatry, addressing the current gap in practical applications.
  • System Building with AI Agents. I am interested in developing sophisticated AI-driven systems capable of simulating realistic patient interactions and facilitating clinical training and assessment. My work includes the design and implementation of interactive AI platforms that can reliably support medical professionals and improve patient care, particularly in Digital and Computational Psychiatry.

From the medical and healthcare perspective, my research concentrates on Neurodegenerative, Neurocognitive, and Neurodevelopmental Disorders, such as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD) and Parkinson’s Disease (PD). I'm also interested in studying digital and AI-driven methods in self harm prevention (e.g., NSSI) and sleep disorders analysis (e.g., iRBD). Specific applications of my research are:

  • Auxiliary Diagnostics and Mechanism Understanding. Leveraging AI and multimodal data integration, my research seeks to enhance early and precise diagnosis of neurological disorders and unravel underlying disease mechanisms through computational modeling and data-driven insights.
  • Digital Intervention and Patient-Facing Applications. Developing innovative digital tools aimed at personalized intervention, continuous monitoring, and improved patient engagement, empowering patients and caregivers through accessible, AI-powered technologies.
